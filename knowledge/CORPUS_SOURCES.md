# UX/UI Design Critique Knowledge Corpus: Authoritative Sources Across 10 Domains

A reference guide for building a grounded AI critique agent library, covering canonical books, research papers, specifications, web resources, and open-source design systems across the core knowledge areas of UX/UI evaluation.

## Domain 1: Visual Hierarchy and Layout

### Gestalt Principles

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Nielsen, J. (2024). *Gestalt Principles for Visual UI Design* | Web article | Contemporary | ✅ Free — https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/gestalt-principles |
| Wertheimer, M. (1923). *Untersuchungen zur Lehre von der Gestalt* | Original research paper | Foundational | 📖 Academic library |
| Johnson, J. (2021). *Designing with the Mind in Mind* (3rd ed.) | Book | Canonical + Contemporary | 💰 Purchase (Elsevier/Morgan Kaufmann) |

The nine Gestalt principles — proximity, similarity, closure, continuity, figure-ground, and others — are essential for designing user interface layouts. Jakob Nielsen's 2024 article on Gestalt for UI is a freely accessible, practitioner-focused synthesis.

### Eye-Tracking and Reading Patterns

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Nielsen, J. (2006). *F-Shaped Pattern For Reading Web Content* | Research article | Foundational | ✅ Free — https://www.nngroup.com/articles/f-shaped-pattern-reading-web-content-discovered/ |
| NNGroup (2017). *F-Pattern in Reading Digital Content* | Video/article update | Contemporary | ✅ Free — https://www.nngroup.com/videos/f-pattern-reading-digital-content/ |
| NNGroup (2026). *4 Eyetracking Patterns: How People Read Online* | LinkedIn/article | Emerging | ✅ Free |

The original NNGroup 2006 study of 232 users found that the F-pattern was the dominant reading pattern across many site types. Updated 2026 research identifies additional patterns: Layer-Cake (heading scanning), Spotted (jumping to bold/linked text), and Commitment (full reading).

### Grid Systems and Typography

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Müller-Brockmann, J. (1981). *Grid Systems in Graphic Design* | Book | Foundational | 💰 Purchase |
| Bringhurst, R. (2004). *The Elements of Typographic Style* (3rd ed.) | Book | Canonical | 💰 Purchase |
| Brown, T. (2011). *More Meaningful Typography*, A List Apart | Web article | Contemporary | ✅ Free — https://alistapart.com/article/more-meaningful-typography/ |

Bringhurst defines a modular scale as "a prearranged set of harmonious proportions" — widely applied to define typographic hierarchies (H1–H6 scales, body text, captions). Tim Brown's A List Apart article operationalises this as the "double-stranded modular scale" methodology for web typography.

## Domain 2: Usability Heuristics and Evaluation Frameworks

### Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Nielsen, J. (1994). *10 Usability Heuristics for User Interface Design* | Original paper | Foundational/Canonical | ✅ Free — https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ten-usability-heuristics/ |
| Nielsen, J. (1994). In: Nielsen & Mack (eds.), *Usability Inspection Methods* | Book chapter | Primary source | 💰 Purchase (Wiley) |
| Nielsen, J. (2024). *How I Developed the 10 Usability Heuristics* | Substack post | Contemporary commentary | ✅ Free — https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/usability-heuristics-history |

The 10 heuristics were derived using factor analysis of 249 usability problems to identify principles with maximum explanatory power. The NNGroup article remains the canonical living reference.

### Shneiderman's Eight Golden Rules

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Shneiderman, B. (1987/2016). *Designing the User Interface* | Book (6th ed.) | Foundational | 💰 Purchase (Pearson) |
| Shneiderman, B. *The Eight Golden Rules of Interface Design* | Web page | Canonical summary | ✅ Free — https://www.cs.umd.edu/users/ben/goldenrules.html |

First introduced in 1986/1987 and applicable to most interactive systems. The University of Maryland page hosts the current authoritative version for free.

### ISO 9241 Usability Standards

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| ISO 9241-11:2018 *Ergonomics of human-system interaction — Usability: Definitions and concepts* | Standard | Authoritative | 💰 Purchase (ISO/SAI) ~$292 AUD |
| ISO 9241-210:2019 *Human-centred design for interactive systems* | Standard | Authoritative | 💰 Purchase |

ISO 9241-11:2018 defines usability as "the extent to which a system, product or service can be used by specified users to achieve specified goals with effectiveness, efficiency and satisfaction in a specified context of use."

### Additional Heuristic Frameworks

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Gerhardt-Powals, J. (1996). *Cognitive engineering principles for enhancing human-computer performance*, IJHCI | Research paper | Supplemental | 📖 Academic library (Taylor & Francis) |
| Weinschenk, S. & Barker, D. (2000). *Designing Effective Speech Interfaces* | Book (contains W&B heuristics) | Supplemental | 💰 Purchase |
| Weinschenk, S. (2020). *100 Things Every Designer Needs to Know About People* (2nd ed.) | Book | Contemporary canonical | 💰 Purchase (New Riders) |

Gerhardt-Powals' cognitive engineering principles focus on mental model compatibility, uncertainty reduction, and minimizing memory load. Empirical studies found no significant differences in effectiveness, efficiency and inter-evaluator reliability compared to Nielsen's heuristics.

### Severity Ratings

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Nielsen, J. (1994/2024). *Severity Ratings for Usability Problems* | Web article | Canonical | ✅ Free — https://www.nngroup.com/articles/how-to-rate-the-severity-of-usability-problems/ |
| MeasuringU. *Rating the Severity of Usability Problems* | Comparative article | Contemporary | ✅ Free — https://measuringu.com/rating-severity/ |

The standard 0–4 scale: 0 = not a problem, 1 = cosmetic, 2 = minor, 3 = major, 4 = usability catastrophe. Combines frequency, impact, and persistence.

## Domain 4: Accessibility (WCAG and Beyond)

### WCAG 2.1 and 2.2

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| W3C (2024). *WCAG 2.2 W3C Recommendation* | Specification | Authoritative | ✅ Free — https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/ |
| W3C (2018). *WCAG 2.1 W3C Recommendation* | Specification | Authoritative (baseline) | ✅ Free — https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG21/ |

WCAG 2.2 (October 2023, updated December 2024) adds nine new success criteria including minimum touch target size (24×24 CSS pixels), focus appearance, consistent help placement, and accessible authentication. Content conforming to WCAG 2.2 also conforms to WCAG 2.0 and 2.1.

### WAI-ARIA

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| W3C WAI. *WAI-ARIA Overview* | Specification overview | Authoritative | ✅ Free — https://www.w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/aria/ |
| W3C WAI. *ARIA Authoring Practices Guide (APG)* | Patterns guide | Authoritative | ✅ Free — https://www.w3.org/WAI/ARIA/apg/ |

WAI-ARIA provides the framework for adding attributes to identify features for user interaction, how they relate to each other, and their current state — essential for dynamic content, custom widgets, and single-page applications.

### Touch Target Sizing

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| NNGroup (2019). *Touch Targets on Touchscreens* | Research article | Canonical | ✅ Free — https://www.nngroup.com/articles/touch-target-size/ |
| MIT Touch Lab (various). Finger dimension research | Primary research | Foundational | 📖 Academic |
| NNGroup. *Fitts's Law and Its Applications in UX* | Web article | Canonical | ✅ Free — https://www.nngroup.com/articles/fitts-law/ |

The MIT Touch Lab found average fingertip widths of 1.6–2cm, with thumbs averaging 2.5cm. NNGroup recommends a minimum of 1cm × 1cm (approximately 44px) as a general rule, grounded in Fitts's Law. Apple and Google both specify 44–48px minimums.

### Cognitive Accessibility (COGA)

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| W3C COGA Task Force. *Making Content Usable for People with Cognitive and Learning Disabilities* | Working Group Note | Authoritative | ✅ Free — https://www.w3.org/TR/coga-usable |
| W3C WAI. *Supplemental Guidance: Cognitive Accessibility* | Guidance | Authoritative | ✅ Free — https://www.w3.org/WAI/cognitive/ |

The eight COGA guidelines address: understanding what things are and how to use them; finding what you need; clear content; avoiding mistakes; focus; memory independence; help and support; and adaptation/personalisation.

### Inclusive Design

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Microsoft. *Inclusive Design Toolkit* | Toolkit (PDFs, activity cards, videos) | Authoritative | ✅ Free — https://inclusive.microsoft.design |

Contains 16 activity cards, 7 support cards, principles, mindsets, 3 booklets, and 14 videos. Core principle: "Inclusive is how we design. It's our tools and methods. In comparison, accessibility offers ways to improve access to what is already designed."

## Domain 6: Design Critique Methodology

### Structured Critique Frameworks

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Lerman, L. & Borstel, J. (2003/2022). *Critique Is Creative: The Critical Response Process* | Book | Canonical | 💰 Purchase (Wesleyan University Press) |
| Lerman, L. *Critical Response Process — The Basics* | One-page PDF | Practical summary | ✅ Free — https://lizlerman.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Critical-Response-Process-in-Brief_CRP-one-pager_updated-2020_03_24.pdf |

Devised by choreographer Liz Lerman in 1990. Its four-step structure uses a facilitator to guide statements of meaning, questions from the artist, neutral questions from responders, and then permissioned opinions.

### Design Review Practices

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| 8th Light (2022). *5 Modes of Critique to Improve Design Reviews* | Web article | Contemporary | ✅ Free — https://8thlight.com/insights/5-modes-of-critique-to-improve-design-reviews |
| NNGroup (2016). *Design Critiques: Encourage a Positive Culture* | Web article | Canonical | ✅ Free — https://www.nngroup.com/articles/design-critiques/ |

The five IDEO modes — inspire, provoke, clarify, nudge, and nip & tuck — correspond to different stages of design fidelity from strategy through delivery. NNGroup identifies two prerequisites for useful critique: clear scope and agreed-upon design objectives.

## Domain 7: Colour Theory and Application in UI

### Colour Psychology

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Elliot, A.J. & Maier, M.A. Color-in-Context Theory research (2007/2014) | Academic papers | Foundational | 📖 Academic library (Psychological Science) |
| Koroveshi, P. (2023). *The Impact of Colour Psychology in User Experience* | Thesis | Contemporary survey | ✅ Free — Haaga-Helia University repository |
| SCIRP (2024). *Applying Psychology to Improve the Use of Color in Digital Interfaces* | Open-access paper | Contemporary | ✅ Free — https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=137279 |

Color responses are moderated by culture, context, saturation, and individual differences — making prescriptive rules risky without testing. Research shows appropriate color contrast can enhance readability by up to 40%.

### ColorBrewer and Data Visualization Colour

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Brewer, C.A., Harrower, M. & Penn State (2002). *ColorBrewer 2.0* | Tool + research | Canonical | ✅ Free — https://colorbrewer2.org |
| Brewer, C.A. & Harrower, M. (2003). *ColorBrewer.org: An Online Tool for Selecting Colour Schemes for Maps*, The Cartographic Journal | Paper | Foundational | 📖 Academic / PDF — https://www.cs.rpi.edu/~cutler/classes/visualization/S18/papers/colorbrewer.pdf |

ColorBrewer provides sequential, diverging, and qualitative palettes tested for CRT, LCD, print, projection, and colorblind accessibility. It is the standard reference for data visualization color selection.

### Dark Mode Design

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Material Design 3 — Dark theme guidelines | Specification | Authoritative | ✅ Free — https://m3.material.io/styles/color/dark-theme |
| Apple HIG — Dark Mode section | Specification | Authoritative | ✅ Free — https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/dark-mode |
| LogRocket Blog (2025). *Dark Mode UI Design: Best Practices* | Web article | Contemporary | ✅ Free — https://blog.logrocket.com/ux-design/dark-mode-ui-design-best-practices-and-examples/ |

Core principle: avoid pure black and pure white — use near-black surfaces and desaturated accent colors to prevent vibration and eye strain. Material Design specifies a minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio for text in dark mode.

## Domain 8: Motion and Animation in UI

### Material Design Motion Guidelines

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Material Design 3 — Motion guidelines | Specification | Authoritative | ✅ Free — https://m3.material.io/styles/motion |
| Google Design (2016/2024). *Making Motion Meaningful* | Web article | Contemporary | ✅ Free — https://design.google/library/making-motion-meaningful |

The M3 motion system covers four transition patterns — container transform, shared axis, fade through, and fade. Duration around 350ms is suggested for standard UI transitions.

### Animation Principles for Interfaces

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Thomas, F. & Johnston, O. (1981). *The Illusion of Life: Disney Animation* | Book/conceptual origin | Foundational | 💰 Purchase |
| Willenskomer, I. *UX in Motion Manifesto* | Web article | Contemporary canonical | ✅ Free — https://medium.com/ux-in-motion/creating-usability-with-motion-the-ux-in-motion-manifesto-a87a4584ddc |
| Google Design (2016). *Making Motion Meaningful* | Web article | Contemporary | ✅ Free — https://design.google/library/making-motion-meaningful |

Google's four principles of motion for UI: informational (communicates change), focused (guides attention), expressive (communicates brand), and efficient (feels quick and responsive).

### Reduced Motion

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| MDN Web Docs. *prefers-reduced-motion CSS media feature* | Technical reference | Authoritative | ✅ Free — https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Reference/At-rules/@media/prefers-reduced-motion |
| WCAG 2.2 SC 2.3.3 Animation from Interactions (AAA) | Specification | Authoritative | ✅ Free — https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/ |

### Thumb Zone Research

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Hoober, S. (2013). *How Do Users Really Hold Mobile Devices?*, UXmatters | Research article | Canonical/Foundational | ✅ Free — https://www.uxmatters.com/mt/archives/2013/02/how-do-users-really-hold-mobile-devices.php |

### Responsive vs. Adaptive Design

| Source | Type | Status | Free? |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Marcotte, E. (2011). *Responsive Web Design* | Book | Canonical/Foundational | 💰 Purchase (A Book Apart) |
| BrowserStack (2025). *Adaptive vs Responsive Design* | Web article | Contemporary reference | ✅ Free — https://www.browserstack.com/guide/adaptive-design-vs-responsive-design |

Responsive uses fluid layouts and CSS media queries for a single codebase; adaptive uses fixed layouts for specific breakpoints. Responsive is favoured by Google for search and easier to maintain.

## Heuristic Filters for Quality Content

When searching these sources, prioritise posts containing:
- "We decided to..." / "We chose to..." + explanation
- "We tested..." + "the results showed..."
- "We initially thought..." + "but then we found..."
- "The constraint was..." / "The trade-off was..."
- "We reversed..." / "We rolled back..." / "We deprecated..."
