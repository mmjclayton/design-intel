## Key Lessons from Design Retrospectives

1. **Intuitive "improvements" often fail** - Booking.com found that changes users say they want frequently reduce performance in tests
2. **Scale breaks early decisions** - Intercom documents how navigation and IA choices that worked at small scale needed redesigning
3. **Design systems require organisational investment** - Etsy's retrospective shows how org structure creates or constrains design quality
4. **Strong defaults beat endless customisation** - Linear's philosophy: encoding best practices into product structure rather than exposing options
5. **Aesthetics matter beyond surface** - Stripe treats design quality as a three-tier concern: does it work, does it look good, does it feel seamless

## Heuristic Filters for Quality Content

When searching these sources, look for posts containing:
- "We decided to..." / "We chose to..." + explanation
- "We tested..." + "the results showed..."
- "We initially thought..." + "but then we found..."
- "The constraint was..." / "The trade-off was..."
- "We reversed..." / "We rolled back..." / "We deprecated..."

These phrases indicate content that explicitly encodes design reasoning.

## When to apply
- Reference specific company approaches when recommending design patterns
- Cite experimentation evidence when challenging intuitive assumptions
- Use retrospective lessons to identify when a design may not scale
- Ground critique in real-world examples of how similar problems were solved
